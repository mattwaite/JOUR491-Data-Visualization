The Robo Effect

Not Your Grandmother's Volleyball Breakdown

By Pat Radigan

In her one-year Husker career, Kelsey Robinson lived up to the hype, delivering Nebraska's first Big Ten Player of the year in addition to helping the Huskers exceed expectations in what some considered a 'rebuilding year'. With the National Player of the Year award set to be announced today, the time has come for us to try and put Robinson's year into perspective, but as Husker coach John Cook is quick to point out, that is no easy task.

"There's just so many ways you can evaluate her. You need to read the book 'Moneyball', she's the Moneyball player," he said. "There are things that statistically show up and there are outside of the box things that show up in other ways."


Well coach, we may not have a Brad Pitt-type character hanging out around the office, but what we did come up with was a way to develop our own theory on what truly makes a player successful. After kicking around the best ways to completely account for a hitter's worth, we eventually developed the "Hitter Performance Rating" to try and put all of Robinson's contributions into one stat, and compare her numbers to the rest of the country:

[Kills - Attack Errors + Service Aces - Service Errors - Return Errors +
(Digs * Team Hit %) + Solo Blocks + (Block Assists/2) - Block Errors] / Sets = HPR
