The Showdown

As you pour over the numbers, both HPR and just simple stat breakdowns, two names keep emerging: Kelsey Robinson and Krista Vansant. Both players embody the concept of a six-rotation player and their numbers show it, so much so that the American Vollleyball Coaches Association used its Twitter profile for an impromptu vote between Robinson and Vansant for the player of the year award. While Robinson won that by a count of 427 retweets to 108, you'll see from the more in-depth breakdown below that it's not just Twitter that makes the case for Robinson to take home the AVCA Player of the Year at today's banquet...

Better When It Counts

Without postseason conference tournaments in college volleyball, the imiportance on the conference portion of the regular season schedule cannot be understated. When looking at the two seasons side-by-side, Vansant's 4.04 HPR in Pac 12 play is on-par with her season total, but it is Robinson who upped her game during conference play, finishing with a 4.60 HPR during Big Ten action. With two Big Ten teams set to play for the national title (and seven crashing this year's Sweet Sixteen), Robinson's effort in conference play is even more impressive, showing just how crucial the senior was in big moments for a Husker team that finished second in the league.

Battle Tested

Taking a quick look at each player's number against ranked opponents, it is actually Vansant's 4.60 HPR against ranked teams that exceed's Robinson's mark of 4.47 in ranked matches, but the trick here is that Robinson has nearly twice as many matchups against ranked opponents as Vansant to her name. In fact, Robinson posted nine of her top 13 single-game efforts of 2013 against ranked opponents, while Vansant has only eight total games against ranked opposition. So yes, Vansant put up an HPR of 4.60 in her eight mathches against nationally ranked foes, but it is Robinson who had eight games of 4.61 or higher in ranked showdowns during the regular season.

Captain Clutch

Building off of the quantity of quality performances by Robinson, there's also a distinct edge in Robo's favor when you look at the quality of opponents in some of her best matches this season. Of Robinson's top three performances by HPR, her top two efforts came in sweeps of No. 19 Michigan (No. 33 RPI) and No. 8 Minnesota (No. 11 RPI), while her third best match of the season was a 6.01 mark against Illinois (No. 20 RPI). For Vansant, her best night out came against Coppin State (No. 250 RPI), while her second-best effort also came in non-conference play against Portland State (No. 83 RPI). Vansant's only top three performance against a conference opponent came against Washington State, however, if the Cougars were in the Big Ten they'd be the 11th best team in the league based on RPI.
