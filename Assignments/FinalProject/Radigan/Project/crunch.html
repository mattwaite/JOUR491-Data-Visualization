Coming Up Clutch...


Anyone who knows anything about sports knows it's not just about the total output of a player, rather, it's about how much they can affect the team. In the above graph, instead of just looking at overall HPR in the Elite Eight round (Robinson finished with the fourth highest total of any hitter with a 5.05 in a loss to Texas), we look at the impact each hitter had on her team. When looking at percentage of team's HPR, Robinson was reponsible for 80 percent of the output among Nebraska's three hitters, the highest percentage of any hitter of the eight teams competing.





And not only did Robinson come up clutch for the Huskers, but her HPR in the match, despite being swept, turned out to be one of the top marks posted by any player in the round of eight.

Outside of the 5.50 of Haley Eckerman to cap an impressive day for Texas' hitters, Robinson's effort against the Longhorns was third best from all eight teams, falling behind a pair of big numbers posted in the game noted below as 'the outlier'. Of the Big Ten teams in the round of eight, Robinson's HPR was the best by a third of a point, showing her ability to produce even in the face of the sweep.

To further show the value of the HPR, you can see the affect of All-Big Ten setter Lauren Carlini in the fact Wisconsin's three hitters finished with totals of 3.67, 3.56 and 3.36 in advancing to the Final Four.

By this point, in addition to the HPR showing signs of withstanding the test of actual data, there's also another trend emerging: Kelsey Robinson delivered when the Huskers needed it the most.

The Outlier: The one issue that does need to be addressed here, especially when you see the two numbers posted in the match, is the existence of an outlier in the form of the Washington vs. USC match. In the third matchup this season of the two teams, both Krista Vansant and Nwanebu used that familiarity to their advantage to take turns lighting each other up while playing on the same pin. Yes, both players put up impressive numbers, but given the circumstances (three total blocks between the two players) it is easy to see where all of the kills and digs came from in what turned into a mano-y-mano showdown in LA.
