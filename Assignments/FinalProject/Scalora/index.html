Poverty and health insurance

As part of the Affordable Care Act, or "Obamacare," states have the opportunity to increase medicaid eligibility to 133 percent of the poverty level, according to the White House. So far, Nebraska has not been one of those states.

25 percent

That's the percentage of counties in Nebraska that have seen an increase in the number of uninsured persons since 2007. Blaine County, in central Nebraska, saw the largest increase, with a 115 percent increase. Gosper County saw a nearly 40 percent decrease in uninsured persons during that time.
